Project Overview

Background

In 2022, I participated in a 48-hour product design hackathon. The challenge was to redesign the Air-Venti app, which aimed to help users track the availability of ICU beds and ventilators. This app in its current form, does not provide a good user experience.

Objectives

Primary Objective:
Redesign the user experience for users of all age groups.

Secondary Objective:
Design a map view displaying hospital locations with ICU bed and ventilator statistics.

Initial Approach

Being new to the design process and lack of comprehensive design knowledge, I dived straight into Figma to craft a solution without conducting thorough research and understanding. My focus centred on creating an intuitive dashboard, refining the search functionality, and introducing a map view feature.

Factors Contributing to an Unsuccessful Solution

1. Lack of user feedback
Being new to the design process and lack of comprehensive design knowledge
2. Incomplete Solution
Being new to the design process and lack of comprehensive design knowledge

Re-Analysis

After two years of gaining valuable knowledge in UX design, I revisited the Air-Venti app challenge with a fresh perspective. The aim was to address the shortcomings identified during the hackathon and apply the knowledge acquired over the past two years to create a more informed and user-centric design.

Issues Identified

  • Poor UI design
  • Inconsistent colour palette
  • Inconsistent spacing between elements
  • Non available beds are equally highlighted
  • No information about pricing
