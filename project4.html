System Evaluated

Our team took a deep dive into the Walmart mobile app, a retail powerhouse that lets users browse, shop, and manage their purchases with ease—or so we thought! While the app is packed with useful features like product searches, shopping cart management, and order tracking, users often run into frustrating hiccups, especially in navigation, performance, and checkout. We wanted to explore these pain points, understand real user frustrations, and come up with solid recommendations for improvement.

Why Walmart?

Walmart’s app is a major player in the retail space, serving millions of users daily. Any usability issue—big or small—directly impacts a vast customer base. By evaluating its user experience, we aimed to uncover problem areas and suggest solutions that could make shopping smoother and more enjoyable.

Our Process

Understanding the User Experience

We kicked things off by talking to real Walmart app users through user interviews. We asked about their shopping habits, what they love, and what drives them crazy. This gave us an understanding of user expectations and common frustrations.

Participant Demographics

We recruited 10 participants (2 per each team member) through online channels, focusing on a diverse representation of Walmart app users:
  • Age Range: 22–33 years, with the majority in the 22–24 age range.
  • Occupations: Included both students (6 users) and working professionals (4 users) to capture varied use cases and experiences.
  • Usage Patterns: Participants ranged from frequent to occasional users, providing insights from new and experienced perspectives.

Data Collection Methods

To evaluate Walmart app's usability, we employed two primary methods:
  • Online Surveys: Distributed to a diverse group of Walmart app users to gather quantitative data on satisfaction and usability issues.
  • Face-to-Face Interviews: Conducted with selected participants, including both households and students, to gain in-depth insights into shopping experiences, challenges, and improvement suggestions.

Insights from the Interviews

Analyzing Data

From the user interview data, we identified several recurring themes and grouped them to create an affinity map. We categorized the pain points into five main areas: unclear checkout process, latency issues, poor help and support, missing features, and navigation challenges.
